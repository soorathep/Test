# SKH Writing Program — Editorial Map

A planning reference for the five essay series on skhgroup.net. The goal: keep every series
distinct, so the same topic can appear in more than one series *deliberately*, from a different
angle — never by accident.

---

## 1. The five series — mode, voice, and signature

Each series is separated by **who speaks, to whom, from when** — not by topic. That is what keeps
them from blurring.

| | Series | Mode / voice | Signature device |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Through the Thinkers | A thinker's lens on research (philosophy) | Open "What X might say"; close "Every morning, a researcher should ask…" |
| **B** | Beyond Metrics | First person, present — self & the system | Open with a common belief, then complicate it; close on a reframe (no daily-question) |
| **C** | Lessons from Science | A lab phenomenon → a life truth (metaphor) | Open with the concrete science; cross to life; close holding both in parallel |
| **D** | Letters to My Younger Self | Present self → past self (retrospective) | "Dear Soorathep, 20XX —"; close with one line from the present |
| **E** | The Craft of Research | Practical how-to for the reader (evergreen) | Open with the failure mode / why it matters; close with a short checklist or principle |

## 4. Three rules to keep them distinct

1. **Separate by voice, not topic.** If two drafts feel alike, the fix is the *mode* (see §1), not a new topic.
2. **One idea, one angle per series.** Use the matrix: assign a shared theme its distinct treatment in each series before writing.
3. **Don't launch all five at once.** A is complete; run **B + C** next, add **D + E** once those have momentum. Quality over count — let each series be open-ended, not locked at 20.
